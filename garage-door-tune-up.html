Why Is My Garage Door So Loud?

A noisy door isn't just annoying; it's a sign that your door is suffering from unnecessary wear and tear. Here are the common culprits.

Worn-Out Metal Rollers

Old, unsealed metal rollers grind fiercely against metal tracks. They clack and rattle as the door moves. Upgrading to sealed nylon rollers instantly silences the tracks.

Bone-Dry Hinges & Springs

Without proper seasonal lubrication, metal grinds on metal. This causes loud popping, squeaking, and shuddering, which can rapidly degrade your hardware.

Unbalanced Tracks & Tension

If your tracks are misaligned or your springs have lost their proper tension, your motor has to work twice as hard, resulting in deep groans and vibration through your walls.

Our Process

The Ultimate Garage Tune-Up

We don't just spray some WD-40 and walk away. Our comprehensive tune-up rebalances your system, tightens loose hardware, and replaces the parts that cause the most racket.

  • Nylon Roller Replacement (Game Changer) We swap out loud metal rollers for commercial-grade sealed nylon rollers. This is the #1 way to make your door whisper-quiet.
  • Professional Grade Lubrication We apply specialized, temperature-resistant silicone and lithium grease to your springs, hinges, bearings, and opener rail.
  • Track Alignment & Hardware Tightening Vibration loosens bolts over time. We go through every hinge and bracket to ensure everything is tight and perfectly aligned.
  • Spring Tension Balancing We test the weight of your door and adjust your torsion springs so your motor doesn't have to strain (and groan) while lifting.
